Park Yeon-mi is a human rights activist who escaped North Korea at the age of 13. After being sold into slavery in China, she and her mother fled to Mongolia by walking across the Gobi desert. They resettled in South Korea in 2009. She is a member of Liberty in North Korea, an organization that rescues North Korean refugees who are living in hiding in China. Park is currently studying for a BA in Economics at Columbia University.
