PETJ

People for the Ethical Treatment of Jackalopes

The world's only antlered lagomorph, jackalopes were once an integral part of the North American desert ecosystem. Thanks to trophy hunting, these special animals are now endangered in the wild. Today, the majority of the jackalope population suffers cruelty and reduced lifespans in captive breeding programs. Our organization works to improve the welfare of both captive and wild jackalopes across North America through both community outreach and direct action. Learn how you can be a part of our mission.

Rehabilitation

Our rehabilitation program works to reintroduce rescued jackalopes into the wild. Our sanctuary serves as a home for jackalopes not suited to release into the wild.

Rescue

Learn about our direct efforts to rescue abused jackalopes from so-called "scientific" research and inhumane breeding programs.

Education & Outreach

We work to dispel harmful myths and rewrite the narrative about these magnificent creatures.
